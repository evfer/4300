Module 5: Advantages, Disadvantages, and Limitations of Resource-Based Theory

Advantages of RBT

  • Focus on internal resources: RBT emphasizes the unique internal resources of a firm over external market conditions (Jugdev, 2004).
  • Sustainable competitive advantage: By highlighting the importance of resources that are valuable, rare, inimitable, and organized (VRIO), RBT enables firms to achieve long-term competitive advantages that are difficult for rivals to replicate (Jugdev, 2004).
  • Strategic resource allocation: Encourages firms to strategically allocate resources to high-value initiatives, encouraging efficient use of assets and improved performance (Ghapanchi et al., 2014).
  • Flexibility across industries: RBT can be applied in different industries, as it focuses on internal capabilities rather than industry-specific items.

Disadvantages of RBT

  • Ignore external factors: RBT emphasizes internal resources and capabilities, but it limits applicability in dynamic environments, where external factors play a big role (Svejvig & Schlichter, 2021).
  • Static perspective: It is assumed that resources remain stable over time; however, in reality, dynamic markets require continuous adaptation (Huang et al., 2020).
  • High resource development costs: Building and sustaining valuable, unique resources can be both expensive and time-consuming.

Limitations of RBT

  • Ignore market dynamics: RBT does not fully consider external market forces, such as economic trends and geopolitical risks. In public healthcare, traditional market forces like economic trends and competition are not as important (Svejvig & Schlichter, 2021). There is a need to adapt RBT to public value instead of profit (Svejvig & Schlichter, 2021).
  • Assumes resource immobility: The theory assumes that resources cannot be easily transferred, though in practice, many resources can be (Jugdev, 2004).

RBT is useful for analyzing internal strengths, however, in volatile industries like tech and finance, firms must be responsive to external pressures such as regulation, consumer shifts, and disruptive innovations. For example, a company that focuses only on internal capabilities may miss market shifts driven by technological breakthroughs or political events.
