Module 6: Application to Project Management

The RBT is a strategic framework that helps organizations gain a competitive edge by leveraging their unique internal resources. In agile project management, RBT provides valuable insights into how companies can optimize their workforce, technology, and knowledge on how to do projects more efficiently and effectively to gain a competitive advantage.

In Agile project management, RBT allows firms to understand how to use their internal capabilities such as team skills, processes, and knowledge to deliver projects more effectively and outperform competitors. Agile practices like continuous development, iterative improvement, and self-organizing teams depend heavily on how well an organization manages and nurtures its internal resources (Leybourne, 2009).

Jugdev states that project management can be a source of sustainable competitive advantage when it's integrated into the organization’s culture, systems, and knowledge base (Jugdev, 2004). It’s more than using standardized project management tools, but intangible resources like trust among team members, leadership, and shared knowledge (Jugdev, 2004). This is difficult to measure and see, making it difficult to replicate. Firms that develop rare, inimitable project management capabilities gain a competitive advantage over other companies (Jugdev, 2004).

Organizational support for project management processes significantly improves both project-level and firm-level performance (Azevedo et al., 2022). The most influential factors in project success are not the rarity or inimitability of the tools, but the value of the processes and how much the organization supports them (Azevedo et al., 2022). Firms that align project management with strategic goals, invest in knowledge-sharing, and create integration across teams are better positioned to achieve sustained competitive advantages (Azevedo et al., 2022).

To effectively apply RBT to project management, firms should:

  • Evaluate their project management resources using the VRIO framework.
  • Invest in building organizational capabilities.
  • Develop knowledge-sharing systems for continuous improvement.
  • Align project management with business goals to ensure it delivers measurable value.

RBT encourages continuous investment in employee development and knowledge-sharing. Agile teams can apply RBT by identifying internal skill gaps, fostering a learning culture, and aligning projects with core strengths (Leybourne, 2009). This approach ensures that the team’s capabilities support short-term goals and contribute to long-term, sustainable success. For example, rather than constantly hiring new talent, organizations can use RBT to identify skill gaps, train existing employees, and build cross-functional teams that evolve with business needs. This aligns with Agile’s emphasis on adaptive learning and constant improvement, ensuring that resources are used strategically to maximize productivity and success (Leybourne, 2009). Overall, Agile’s emphasis on adaptability, team empowerment, and iterative improvement naturally aligns with RBT’s goal of turning internal resources into strategic assets (Leybourne, 2009).
